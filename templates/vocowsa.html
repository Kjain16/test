About

VOCOWSA (VOice COntrolled Wheelchair Semi-Autonomous) is a prototype of a simple voice controlled wheelchair developed by the students at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, which moves according to the commands of the user and applies obstacle avoidance i.e, smartly tackles to manoeuvre around obstacles that come in its path.

The main motivation behind this project is to create an innovative and creative solution for a low cost smart wheelchair for specially abled people. Through this fusion of technology and innovation, we want to reach out to those billions of needy people out there who have nearly no access to such assistive devices, mainly because of their high costs.

The general framework of VOCOWSA consists of Arduino, Raspberry pi, HM2007 voice module, ultrasonic sensors and a digital compass. We have also implemented artificial intelligence in VOCOWSA, however at a rudimentary level.

Implementation

VOCOWSA has two modes of operation. In the first mode, or ‘sensing mode’, VOCOWSA constantly collects data from the environment through ultrasonic sensors and digital compass and gains information about the obstacles present around it. In the second mode i.e, the Navigation Mode, VOCOWSA makes smart decisions and navigates autonomously around obstacles. This in turn helps to share the control with the user, resulting in safer navigation. It also helps to reduce the user’s low-level involvement in controlling VOCOWSA.

The majority of the data analysis and decision-making takes place in the Raspberry-pi placed on the base of VOCOWSA, which serves as the heart of the integrated system. The raspi communicates with the Arduino via a serial connection that is set at a baud rate of 9600. Through this connection, it receives the user’s command. The raspi is also connected to 4 sonar sensors through the GPIO pins, with each sensor situated at the midpoint of VOCOWSA’s 4 sides. These sensors constantly keep taking readings and update the raspi about VOCOWSA’s surroundings. These readings play a crucial role in other future activities of VOCOWSA. The raspi then makes a decision based on the user’s command and the corresponding sensor’s reading and instructs VOCOWSA to move accordingly.

Specifications

    Many interesting observations were made during the actualization of VOCOWSA.
  • VOCOWSA moved at a speed of 0.635m/s with a pay load of 10kgs, more effective than other wheelchairs nowadays which move at 0.6 m/s.
  • VOCOWSA has been implemented 3x3 sq.ft base which too moved at a speed of 0.6m/s with a practical payload of 70kgs.
  • During its rotation about the hind wheels, VOCOWSA rotated 18 degrees every second with a rotational error of about 3 degree 5 minutes.
  • The braking distance of VOCOWSA was observed to be about 26.67 cm.
  • The minimum clearance limit for any obstacle is 30 cm i.e, while approaching any obstacle VOCOWSA stops within a distance of 30 cm from the obstacle.
  • The turning radius of VOCOWSA is around 49.57 cm.
  • The maximum size of the chair that can be placed on VOCOWSA is 20 inches by 20 inches and the maximum pay load that it can sustain is 15kgs.
  • Any sensor of VOCOWSA takes 2.5 s to complete a 180 degree sweep i.e, a full scan of the surroundings along a particular side.
  • Each sensor gives about 9 readings in 2 seconds. The raspi then takes about 1.2 sec + or – 11 milliseconds to process this data and send the appropriate command to the motor driver.

Future Extensions

  • Apply NLP to the voice module to make it completely user independent.
  • Apply Machine Learning algorithms (in RasPi 3) to make it completely autonomous and capable of simultaneously locating and mapping the unknown indoor environment around it.
  • Apply Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning to learn the user's habits and reduce the user's involvement in controlling the wheelchair to maximum level possible .
