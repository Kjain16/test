About Us

Society of Automotive Engineers, IIT (BHU), a collegiate chapter of SAE INDIA was established in the year 2009. Registered to SAE International, it is a platform where students sharing a vehement passion for automobiles sciences involve themselves in various projects. It was established under the patronage of our Late Prof. B.B. Bansal, under the banner of Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT (BHU). The SAE collegiate Club promotes automotive activities in the institutes and has dedicated teams not only for operating club activities but also for participating in SAE BAJA, SAE EFFICYCLE. As has been corroborated in the previous years, students have been working as a strong team with great efforts not only on amassing knowledge and experience in technical field of design and manufacturing, but also in financial and administrative domains.

Vision

Our vision is to bring together students from all segments of engineering, to a common platform, for the purpose of learning and understanding the basics of automobiles and automobile industry. Our aim is to set up an atmosphere for promotion of technical skills by providing industrial experience. Enhancement of practical knowledge through projects relating to real life examples would be our primary focus.

We would be organizing highly interactive sessions with professionals from this fieldby collaborating with various committees and renowned industries. Our goal will be to enhance formal education and professional development. Also, we will be conducting various career enhancement sessions.

Activities

  • Baja SAE - It is an inter-collegiate event hosted by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE) at national level. The students function as a team to design, build, test, promote and compete with an all terrain vehicle within the limits of the rules. The competition is organized in two stages, the first stage being the Virtual Baja competition held in July every year. This phase of the competition consists of designing the vehicle and testing it in real world by simulating the conditions on SAE approved CAD and simulation softwares. This design has to be presented to a jury panel, where it is judged on various parameters viz. economy of design, its response to various impacts and stresses, material used, etc. The second phase consists of the race that takes place in January, where the manufactured vehicle has to undergo various tests like acceleration test, hill climb test, braking test, maneuverability test, endurance test.

  • Effi-cycle - It is a design competition in which engineering students are given a stimulating task of designing, simulating and fabricating human powered plus electric motor. The objective of this event is to encourage people to use green vehicles and at the same time provide a platform for engineering students to tackle problems of the real world with their technical knowledge.

  • Workshops and quizzes are organized on a regular basis with the aim of introducing the key aspects of automobile engineering, detailed information about parts and functioning of a automobile and simulation softwares.

  • Potentio - A pre-Technex event focusing on basic kinematics and rigid body mechanics was organised last year. The task was basically to brush up old physics concepts and use it to make a Mechanical Bot which can play Basketball.

  • Axelerate - An IC-RC car racing event on off-road dirt track with obstacles, is held in Technex every year. This competition involves fabricating a 1:8 RC car with 4.5 cc engine which runs on nitro-methane fuel.
