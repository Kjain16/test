About Us

Club of Programmers(IIT-BHU) is the club under the Science and Technology Council which is responsible for the programming activities and the propogation of knowledge in the fields of Computer Science like Competitive Programming, Machine Learning, Information Security, App Development, Artificial Intelligence, Computational Finance etc.

The club has functional units for different fields of CS, named groups. The following groups work together for the betterment of the culture:-

  • Competitive Programming Group
  • Development Group
  • InfoSec Group
  • Machine Learning Group
  • Group for Computational Finance

All these groups hold internal workshops for the group members and external workshops for the whole institute and spread information and awareness regarding their fields. These groups undertake projects internally and also co-ordinate with other groups for pursuing large scale projects which prove beneficial for the institute.

Vision

To develop the coding culture around the institute by organising large scale workshops on topics like Competitive Programming, App Development, etc.

To increase participation across all branches and especially among girls.

To increase awareness about advanced topics in Computer Science like Quantum Computing, Cloud Computing, Deep Learning, etc.

To conduct more onsite events like hackathons, ICPC-style contests and seminars for research.

To induce awareness about research in Computer Science and motivate students to pursue research.

To pursue large scale projects and create products which will help in the development of student body and the institute.

Achievements

  • A team of 4 members secured 4th position and defeated all teams from other IIT's in novelty in the 1st Inter-IIT-Tech Meet.
  • 3 teams qualified for the India Finals of the ACM-ICPC Indian Regionals and more than 10 teams qualified for the Amritapuri, Chennai, Kolkata and Kharagpur Regionals.
  • Around 7 papers were accepted in International Conferences by the members of the club and many others were accepted in Indian Conferences.
  • 3 teams bagged the 1st, 2nd and 3rd prize in the Quantum Code event organised by Techfest, IIT Bombay.
  • 2 teams were ranked 2nd and 3rd across all IIT's in Goldman Sachs's coding competition "Quantify 16" and 6 teams were selected as Significant Contributors.
  • Ayush Kedia, a core member of the club won the second place in the Tata DoCoMo sponsored Code-sparta, an inter-IIT coding competition and won a prize money of Rs.60000.
  • Pranjal Jain and Divyam Goyal, members of the club won 2nd and 3rd place in the flagship coding event "Humblefool Cup" of the techfest "Aporaksha" of IIIT-Allahabad.

Projects

  • Development of an online judge
  • Predicting solar energy based on weather data
  • Voice recognition
  • Data retrieval on Sanskrit text collection
  • High utility pattern mining in incremental datasets
  • Finding authors of unknown texts
  • Intelligent Chatbot

Activities

  • COPS organised a basic workshop on basic computer programming. It was backed up with two workshops on Competitive Programming and two workshops on Android Development in the odd semester.
  • A contest (COPSOPC-1) was held for the preparation for jobs, then a programming contest was held for freshers namely NP-Compete.
  • An onsite team event for ACM-ICPC was held and it witnessed large scale participation.
  • Two workshops on Machine Learning and three workshops on Information Security were held in the even semester.
  • Code.Fun.Do, a day long appathon organised by Microsoft with COPS was held in the even semester which saw huge participation across several branches from all the 4 years.

Workshops

  • The eigth workshop was held on 12th January, 2017 on Introduction to Machine Learning and Linear Regression.
  • The ninth workshop was held on 25th January, 2017 on Logistic Regression, Clustering and Object Detection.
  • The tenth workshop was held on 26th January, 2017 on Capture the Flag.
